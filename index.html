Gregory Clark

Journalist, ambassador, professor, farmer

Life

  • 1936: Born in Cambridge, United Kingdom.
  • 1953-56: Studied Geography and Ethnology at Oxford University.
  • 1956-65: Australian Department of External Affairs, Canberra.
  • 1959-62: Australian Commission, Hong Kong – Second Secretary (1961, Diploma in Chinese Studies, University of Hong Kong).
  • 1962-63: China Desk Officer, Canberra.
  • 1963-65: Australian Embassy, Moscow – First Secretary.
  • 1965-69: Department of Economics, Research School of Pacific Studies, Australian National University (PhD research on Japanese private direct investment abroad).
  • 1967-68: Hitotsubashi University and Institute of Developing Economies, Tokyo (field research).
  • 1969-74: Head of the Tokyo Office, 'The Australian' (national newspaper).
  • 1974-76: Consultant (Deputy Secretary level), Policy Coordination Unit, Department of the Prime Minister and Cabinet, Canberra.
  • 1976-78: Visiting Professor, Sophia University, Tokyo.
  • 1979-84: Full Professor, Faculty of Economics, Sophia University (Yotsuya campus).
  • 1979-95: Professor, Faculty of Comparative Culture, Sophia University (Ichigaya campus).
  • 1990-2001: Chancellor, Advanced School of the Institute of Developing Economies, Tokyo (Advisor, 2001 – Present).
  • 1995-2001: President, Tama University, Tokyo.
  • 2001-2007: President Emeritus, Tama University, Tokyo.
  • 2001-2010: Honorary Ambassador to Japan, Government of Queensland.
  • 2004-2008: Vice President, Akita International University.
  • 2007-2009: Advisory Member, Mitsu Corp., Strategic Research Institute.

Languages

  • Chinese and Russian (both studied at the UK Foreign Office, interpreter grade A level).
  • Japanese (fully fluent).
  • French
  • Spanish

Awards

  • 1985: Officer of the Order of Leopold II (Belgium)
  • 1990: Tokyo Metropolitan Culture Award
  • 2003: JETRO Award for contributions to understanding Japan

Books

  • 1968: ‘In Fear of China’, Lansdowne Press, Melbourne (translated as ‘Kokusai Seiji to Chugoku’, 1969, Institute of Developing Economies, Tokyo)
  • 1977: ‘Nihonjin: Yuniikusa no Gensen’ (The Japanese: Origins of Uniqueness), Simul Press, Tokyo
  • 1980: ‘Yuniiku na Nihonjin’ (The Unusual Japanese), Kodansha, Tokyo (with Takemura Kenichi)
  • 1981: ‘Naigaijin ga Mita Nihon’ (Japan Seen from Inside and Out), Shuei Shobo, Tokyo (with Hasegawa Keitaro)
  • 1982: ‘Understanding the Japanese’, Kinseido, Tokyo
  • 1990: ‘Gokai sareru Nihonjin’ (The Misunderstood Japanese), Kodansha, Tokyo
  • 1993: ‘The Code-Breaking Technique for Learning Foreign Languages’, Dobunshoin, Tokyo
  • 1996: ‘Eigo Benkyo Kakumei’ (Revolution in English Learning), Goma Shobo, Tokyo
  • 2004: ‘Naze Nihon no Kyoiku wa Kawaranai no ka’ (Why Japanese Education Doesn't Change), Toyo Keizai, Tokyo

Today

Gregory Clark retired from academic life in 2001 and moved to Ohara, Chiba, where he has been growing kiwis and renovating his property with the help of volunteers.

Although he has stopped writing books, he remains a fierce critic of Japan’s foreign policy and education. Thanks to his renovation project through the Workaway platform, many people can spend time in Japan learning about the culture and sharing great moments with him and other volunteers.
